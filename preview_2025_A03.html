Materials can be adapted by using different techniques such as adding, deleting, modifying, and reordering. For example, we can add materials when a language item is not covered sufficiently in the original materials. Materials that are too easy or difficult for learners can be deleted. Modifying can be used to make them more relevant to students' interests and backgrounds and to restructure classroom management. Reordering the sequence of activities is another technique, which includes separating items and regrouping them.

Consider the original material extracted from a grammar exercise book and its adapted version below. In the adapted version, the original exercise has been adapted by using the ① technique.

Original Material
➊
Describe the man's routine in four sentences.
Individual Work
➋
Answer the following questions.
Individual Work
What time do you wake up?
What do you usually wear to work?
What do you usually cook for dinner?
Adapted Material
➊
Describe the student's routine in four sentences. 변경
Individual Work
➋
Work in pairs and ask each other the following questions. 변경
Pair Work
What time do you wake up?
What do you usually wear on school days? 변경

개념 정리
Materials Adaptation Techniques (교재 수정 기법) ① Adding: supplementing a language item not covered sufficiently in the original materials (원본에서 충분히 다뤄지지 않은 언어 항목을 보충) ② Deleting: removing content that is too easy or too difficult for learners (학습자에게 너무 쉽거나 어려운 내용 제거) ③ Modifying: (a) making content more relevant to students' interests and backgrounds; (b) restructuring classroom management (내용을 학습자 관심사·배경에 맞게 변경; 교실 운영 방식 재구성) ④ Reordering: changing the sequence of activities, including separating items and regrouping them (활동 순서 변경 — 분리·재묶음 포함) Key distinctions in this problem: · Same number of items → adding / deleting excluded (항목 수 동일) · Same activity order → reordering excluded (순서 동일) · Content studentification + Pair Work = modifying — both criteria satisfied (내용 학생화 + Pair Work 전환 → modifying 두 기준 동시 충족)
